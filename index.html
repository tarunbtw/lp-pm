What is Polymarket

A market for every outcome

Polymarket is a decentralized prediction market built on Polygon. Anyone can buy shares in the outcome of real-world events — elections, economics, sports, science, crypto, and more.

Each market is a binary question. If you buy "Yes" shares and the answer resolves Yes, your shares pay out $1 each. If you're wrong, they're worth $0. The price at any moment reflects the market's collective probability.

It's not gambling in the traditional sense — it's information aggregation. Prices here have predicted elections more accurately than major pollsters.

How it works

Four steps to trade the truth

01
Connect your wallet
Sign up with email or connect a crypto wallet (MetaMask, Coinbase, etc.). Deposit USDC to fund your account — Polygon keeps gas fees near zero.
02
Pick a market
Browse hundreds of open questions on politics, science, sports, AI, and economics. Each shows the current probability price, volume, and end date.
03
Buy Yes or No
Buy Yes shares if you think the probability is too low, or No shares if you think it's too high. Your price is your implied probability. Sell anytime before resolution.
04
Collect your payout
When the event happens, an oracle resolves the market. Correct shares pay $1.00 each. Funds settle automatically to your wallet in USDC.

Why Polymarket

Built for serious participants

Real money, real skin
Unlike polls or surveys, traders put actual capital on the line. This makes prices genuinely informative — people research before betting.
Deep liquidity
Major political and macro markets routinely exceed $50M in volume. Tight spreads mean you enter and exit positions efficiently.
Anytime liquidity
You don't have to hold until resolution. Sell your position at any time if the market moves in your favor — or to cut losses early.
Non-custodial
Built on Polygon, your funds sit in smart contracts. Polymarket never holds your money in a centralized pool — you keep custody via your wallet.
Transparent resolution
Markets resolve via UMA Protocol's optimistic oracle — a decentralized dispute system where resolutions are publicly verifiable on-chain.
Near-zero fees
Polygon's L2 infrastructure means sub-cent gas fees. Polymarket takes a small cut only on winning trades — no subscription, no deposit fee.
